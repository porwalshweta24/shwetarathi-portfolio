Exploring the Beauty and Bravery of Chittorgarh

Jodhpur
Jodhpur,Rajasthan

My in-laws' family lives in Jodhpur, so I’ve had the pleasure of exploring this vibrant city many times. Each visit to Jodhpur feels like a new adventure as we delve into its rich heritage and vibrant culture. Iconic landmarks such as Mehrangarh Fort, Jaswant Thada, and Shitla Mata Mandir never fail to captivate us with their grandeur and historical significance. We often find ourselves marveling at the beauty of Toorji Ka Jhalra and enjoying the serene ambiance of Kaylana Lake, while the regal Umaid Palace adds a touch of splendor to our explorations.

One of the highlights of our trips is undoubtedly the food. Jodhpurites have a deep love for their local delicacies, and my family is no exception. We make it a point to indulge in the city's culinary delights, starting with the sweet gulab jamuns from Chaturbhuj, which are a true treat for the senses. Vaishnav's makkhan bada is another favorite, offering a rich and satisfying flavor that keeps us coming back for more. We also never miss the chance to savor the delectable ghewar and the crispy jalebi from Janta Sweets, all conveniently located near the city center.

Exploring Jodhpur with my family is always a delightful experience, filled with cherished memories and new discoveries. The city's charm lies not only in its majestic monuments and scenic spots but also in its warm and welcoming people and the unique flavors of its traditional cuisine. Each visit leaves us with a deeper appreciation for Jodhpur and a longing to return and explore even more.

A Journey of Faith: Exploring Charbhujaji and Nathdwara's Srinathji
Charbhujaji- Nathdwara's Srinathji- the Statue of Belief

As a child, the stories of devotion and miracles surrounding Charbhujaji and Nathdwara's Srinathji filled my heart with wonder. Growing up, these sacred places became an integral part of my life, each visit deepening my connection to faith and spirituality. However, it was last year, after Diwali, that I embarked on a journey with my in-laws' family to these holy sites, marking an unforgettable chapter in our lives.

Nestled in the picturesque town of Nathdwara, Charbhujaji Temple stands as a testament to centuries-old devotion. Legend has it that the idol of Lord Vishnu, known as Charbhujaji, miraculously appeared before a devout devotee, Charan Das. The temple, adorned with intricate carvings and vibrant colors, exudes a sense of tranquility that washes over you as soon as you step inside. With each darshan, I felt a profound sense of peace and gratitude, as if the divine presence of Lord Vishnu enveloped me in his embrace.

Our journey then led us to Nathdwara's renowned Srinathji Temple, a place where faith transcends time. The statue of Lord Krishna, adorned with jewels and silks, stands as a symbol of unwavering devotion. As we joined the throngs of devotees in offering prayers and performing rituals, I couldn't help but marvel at the collective spirit of faith that permeated the air. It was as if each prayer echoed through the halls, weaving a tapestry of devotion that bound us all together.

Amidst the hustle and bustle of our pilgrimage, there were moments of serenity and reflection. Whether it was witnessing the aarti ceremony at dawn or simply sitting in quiet contemplation, each experience left an indelible mark on my soul. And as we bid farewell to these sacred sites, I carried with me not only memories but also a renewed sense of faith and purpose.

But our journey didn't end there. On our way back, we made a stop at the Statue of Belief, a towering monument that stands as a beacon of hope and unity. As I gazed up at the majestic figure, my heart swelled with awe and reverence. The statue, with its outstretched arms, seemed to embrace all who stood before it, transcending differences and inviting us to embrace the power of belief.

For me, Charbhujaji, Nathdwara's Srinathji, and the Statue of Belief are more than just landmarks; they are symbols of the enduring power of faith. And as I reflect on our pilgrimage, I am reminded that true belief knows no boundaries, uniting us in a shared journey of devotion and spiritual growth.

Kumbhalgarh
Kumbhalgarh, Rajsamband

When I was studying in college for my graduation, we went on a memorable picnic to Kumbhalgarh with our classmates and lecturers. The bus ride was lively, filled with songs and laughter as we made our way to the majestic fort. Upon arrival, we were awestruck by the breathtaking beauty of Kumbhalgarh, with its lush greenery and historical significance.

We started with a tour of the fort, guided by one of our lecturers who shared fascinating stories about its history. Climbing the steep steps and wandering through ancient corridors, we marveled at the intricate carvings and panoramic views from the top. The sight of the Aravalli Range stretching out before us was simply mesmerizing.

In the afternoon, we found a perfect spot for our picnic under the shade of a large tree. We spread out our mats, shared a variety of delicious homemade snacks, and played games. The camaraderie and laughter made the day special, with even our lecturers joining in the fun.

As the sun began to set, we gathered near the fort walls to watch the sky change colors. The orange and pink hues reflected on the fort's stone walls, creating a magical atmosphere. We boarded the bus to head back to our college, tired but happy, cherishing the unforgettable memories made together at Kumbhalgarh.

Family Trip to Rameshwaram
Chennai-Tirupati-Madurai-Rameshwaram-Kanyakumari-Trivendram

In December 2022, my husband, our nine-month-old daughter, my parents-in-law, and I embarked on an unforgettable journey. We traveled to Chennai, where we roamed around the vibrant city, soaking in its rich culture and history. Our first day trip took us to the sacred Tirupati Balaji, a place of profound spirituality.

The following day, we visited the mesmerizing Vellore Golden Temple. The temple's breathtaking golden facade and serene ambiance left us in awe. After exploring Vellore, we returned to Chennai, where we prepared for our next adventure.

We then set off for Rameshwaram, enjoying a scenic train journey over the Pamban Bridge, which spans the sea. The journey itself was an exhilarating experience, offering stunning views of the ocean and the unique thrill of traveling over water.

Rameshwaram, a place steeped in myth and legend, welcomed us with its sacred aura. We performed the 22 Kund Snan, a ritual bath in the holy water tanks, and marveled at the architectural wonder of the Rameshwaram Temple, with its 1212 intricately carved pillars. We also experienced the divine Mani Darshan, Dhenu Darshan, and witnessed the grand Parvati Mata Palki procession.

During our stay in Rameshwaram, we made a memorable excursion to Dhanushkodi. This ghost town, once a bustling port, was destroyed in a cyclone in 1964. The remnants of buildings and the eerie silence gave the place an ethereal charm. Standing at the confluence of the Bay of Bengal and the Indian Ocean, Dhanushkodi offered stunning views and a sense of tranquility that contrasted with its tragic past.

After three days and two nights in Rameshwaram, we traveled to Madurai to visit the magnificent Meenakshi Temple, dedicated to the goddess Meenakshi. The temple's towering gopurams and vibrant sculptures were a sight to behold.

Our journey continued to Kanyakumari, where we spent a night. We were captivated by the beautiful sunrise over the meeting point of three oceans and visited the iconic Vivekananda Rock Memorial. The serene and picturesque landscape of Kanyakumari was a perfect end to our coastal exploration.

Finally, we traveled to Trivandrum, where we visited the revered Swaminarayan Temple. Our trip concluded with a sense of fulfillment and spiritual enrichment, having visited some of the most sacred and beautiful places in South India.

Family Trip to Maharashtra Jyotirling
Trimbakeshwar - Bhimashankar - Grishneshwar

In the sacred month of Shravan, during the auspicious Adhik Maas of 2023, my husband, our 1.5-year-old daughter, my mother, my brother, and I embarked on a spiritually enriching two-day pilgrimage. Our journey began with a drive to the revered Bhimashankar Temple. As we ascended the hills, the weather turned delightfully foggy, and a gentle rain began to fall, adding a mystical aura to our surroundings. The temple, shrouded in mist, felt like a gateway to another world. We offered our prayers to Lord Shiva, feeling a profound sense of peace and devotion.

After our serene visit to Bhimashankar, we continued our journey to Trimbak. We reached in the evening and decided to rest overnight. The next morning, we woke up early to visit the Jyotirlinga Trimbakeshwar Temple. The temple, nestled in the Western Ghats, exuded an ancient charm. The early morning air was crisp and refreshing, and the temple’s sacred atmosphere filled us with reverence. We performed our rituals and sought blessings, cherishing the tranquility of the moment.

With our hearts full, we proceeded to Grishneshwar, another Jyotirlinga, located near Ellora. The temple, though smaller than the others, was equally powerful in its spiritual presence. We were captivated by its simplicity and the profound devotion it inspired. After offering our prayers and spending some reflective moments, we set off on our journey back home to Pune.

The trip, though brief, was filled with divine experiences and cherished family moments. The foggy weather, the soothing rain, and the sacred temples made our pilgrimage unforgettable, leaving us with a deep sense of gratitude and spiritual fulfillment.

Ashtvinayak Trip
Asht vinayak trip to Maharashtra

Embarking on a two-day journey to the sacred Ashtavinayak temples was a truly enriching experience. Each temple, with its unique history and spiritual significance, left a profound impact on our hearts and souls.

Our pilgrimage began at the Moreshwar Temple in Morgaon, where we felt the divine presence of Lord Ganesha as we offered our prayers and sought blessings for our journey ahead. From there, we traveled to the Siddhivinayak Temple in Siddhatek, where the serene ambiance and the aura of spirituality filled us with peace and tranquility.

The Ballaleshwar Temple in Pali welcomed us with its ancient charm and timeless devotion, while the VaradVinayak Temple in Mahad offered solace and reassurance in the midst of life's challenges. Each temple visit was a reminder of the omnipresent grace of Lord Ganesha, guiding us through every step of our journey.

The Chintamani Temple in Theur, the Girijatmaj Temple in Lenyadri, the Vighnahar Temple in Ozar, and the Mahaganapati Temple in Ranjangaon each held a special place in our hearts. As we offered our prayers at each temple, we felt a deep sense of connection to the divine and to each other, strengthened by the bonds of faith and devotion.

As we concluded our pilgrimage and headed back home, we carried with us the blessings and memories of our time spent in the divine presence of Lord Ganesha.
